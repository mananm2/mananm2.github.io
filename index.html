I am a first-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC). I am a Research Assistant at Distributed Autonomous Systems Laboratory (DASLab) where I work with my advisor, Prof. Girish Chowdhary, on Learning-Based Robotics. I graduated with a MSCS degree from UIUC in 2022. My master's thesis was on "Visual Servoing for Pose Control of Soft Continuum Arms." Previously, I was a research assistant at the University of Notre Dame where I was advised by Prof. Zhiyong Zhang.

I am the acting treasurer of the graduate Society of Women Engineers (GradSWE) at UIUC. I am a strong advocate for women in STEM and enjoy working towards this cause.

Alongside my interest in research, my hobbies include painting, travelling, hiking, and reading.

Research

My primary research interests lie in Learning-Based Robotics, especially, in Agricultural Robotics where manipulator-control is still an open challenge. My ongoing research focuses on the manipulation and control of Soft Continuum Arms using Computer Vision for use in autonomous harvesting.

Contact me if you want to discuss any interesting research idea, or collaborate on something!
